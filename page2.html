Journeying with the Lil' Ones


Not Quite a Bed of Roses

They can be a (very) stinky bunch! Osmeterium (the reddish-orange noodles you see protruding from its head) is a defensive gland that produces a pungent smell to ward off predators. And the smell lingers (for really long) and stays on you even after soaping...


The Painful Truth

Sometimes, deformities cannot be prevented. I have had butterflies with buckled wing tips and have trimmed both wings to eliminate the bent bits, with some success. Those who cannot be salvaged had to be euthanized and my preferred method (others have other ways) is to put it in a container in the freezer, where its system will shut down. :-(


On a Brighter Note

Generally, caterpillars are easy to maintain with a very high success rate! Depending on the species, breeding may be a little tricky. However, through experience, you would know what is involved in looking after butterflies.


The Joy it Brings

That sweet satisfaction when your lil' ones complete metamorphosis! It brings so much joy and excitement as you follow the caterpillar in its growth till its release.
